# Model Catalog (May 2026)

1. Catalog of Major Image and Video Generation Models

The concept of censorship within artificial intelligence image generation operates along a complex spectrum rather than a binary toggle. In the current community consensus, censorship profiles are categorized into distinct tiers. At the most unrestricted end lies the "Unbound" or "WTF" tier, wherein an extreme lack of alignment causes the model to hallucinate explicit or inappropriate content even without explicit prompting.1 The "Completely Uncensored" tier generates exactly what is prompted, including Not Safe For Work (NSFW) content, gore, or political satire, without hesitation or quality degradation.1 The "Minimal" tier lacks active refusal mechanisms, but because its pre-training dataset was cleansed of explicit anatomy, it requires community fine-tuning to generate such concepts accurately.1 "Partial" or "Architectural" censorship indicates the presence of aggressive safety classifiers or a latent space so thoroughly scrubbed that generating specific concepts is nearly impossible.1 Finally, the "Strict" tier involves models that actively refuse prompts based on hardcoded blocklists.2

1.1 The Danbooru Lineage: Anime and Stylized Mastery

The anime and stylized illustration sector remains a highly active and deeply entrenched facet of local generation. This persistence is driven by massive, meticulously tagged datasets (such as Danbooru and e621) and highly dedicated fine-tuning communities that refuse to abandon flexible architectures.

Pony Diffusion Ecosystem (V6 XL and V7) The Pony Diffusion family, authored by AstraliteHeart (PurpleSmart.ai), remains the dominant uncensored general-purpose model for stylized content. Pony Diffusion V6 XL, built upon the SDXL 1.0 base architecture, achieved legendary status due to its unparalleled understanding of complex character interactions, precise anatomical accuracy in explicit scenes, and a massive ecosystem of Low-Rank Adaptations (LoRAs).4 By utilizing a specialized tagging syntax—specifically rating tags such as score_9, score_8_up—it bypasses the limitations of natural language processing to achieve exact compositional control.4

As of early 2026, the highly anticipated Pony Diffusion V7 has entered its deployment phase.6 Bypassing Stability AI’s SD3 line entirely, V7 is built upon the AuraFlow architecture.6 This architectural leap offers native generation up to 1536x1536 resolution, vastly improved prompt comprehension regarding spatial relationships, and enhanced rendering of extreme lighting conditions without color degradation.6 Crucially, Pony V7 remains entirely uncensored. Despite adopting a T5 text encoder—which the community historically feared would limit NSFW capabilities due to its natural language focus—the developers conducted extensive mature-captioning improvements to maintain complete anatomical flexibility.6 The model is highly optimized, capable of running via 8-bit inference or down to 4-bit GGUF quantization for users with severe VRAM constraints.6 Following a period of beta testing on cloud platforms, the open weights are actively being released to the community.7

Illustrious XL and NoobAI-XL While Pony Diffusion excels in versatility and explicit anatomical interaction, the Illustrious XL family, authored by OnomaAI Research, dominates the pure, high-fidelity studio anime aesthetic.4 Based on the SDXL architecture and trained heavily on the Danbooru 2023 dataset, Illustrious XL possesses a deep latent understanding of over 16,000 specific artist styles and character traits.10

NoobAI-XL, developed by Laxhar Lab, emerged as a community darling by fine-tuning the Illustrious XL base using a novel V-Prediction methodology rather than the standard EPS-Prediction utilized by base SDXL.12 This architectural tweak resulted in less mathematical loss during training, yielding superior prompt adherence and stylistic range.12 The most recent variants, such as Chenkin Noob v.03 (updated with datasets through January 2026) and NoobAI-XL-XIN, are widely considered the pinnacle of the SDXL era for anime generation.9 Both model families are fully uncensored and boast cross-compatibility with a vast array of existing SDXL anime LoRAs.13

Anima Released in early 2026 by Circlestone Labs and Comfy Org, Anima is a next-generation, lightweight anime model designed to replace aging SDXL workflows.1 With only 2 billion parameters (2B DiT modified from Cosmos-Predict), it is exceptionally fast and requires minimal VRAM, fitting comfortably on 8GB GPUs.1 It utilizes the Qwen3-0.6B text encoder, allowing users to prompt using targeted natural language programming concepts rather than relying solely on keyword tags.1 Its censorship status is effectively non-existent; the community labels it as "WTF" or "Unbound," noting its tendency to generate explicit content effortlessly.1

1.2 The Transformer Heavyweights: Photorealism and Complexity

The FLUX.1 and FLUX.2 Ecosystem Black Forest Labs redefined open-weight generation with FLUX.1 in August 2024 and subsequently disrupted the market again with FLUX.2 in November 2025.17 FLUX models utilize a Multimodal Diffusion Transformer (MMDiT) flow-matching framework combined with a massive Vision-Language Model (VLM) backbone, virtually eliminating spatial hallucinations and anatomical distortions.17

The FLUX.1 generation was segmented into three primary variants:

Schnell: A distilled, highly optimized variant licensed under Apache 2.0, allowing for unrestricted commercial use.5

Dev: An open-weight, 12B parameter model restricted by a non-commercial license, which became the foundation for community fine-tuning.5

Pro: A closed-source, API-only model optimized for enterprise applications.5

The subsequent FLUX.2 release expanded the architecture significantly, segmenting by parameter scale rather than just licensing:

FLUX.2 [dev] / [pro]: A 32-billion parameter titan requiring up to 90GB of VRAM in unoptimized FP16, though it can be compressed into 24GB GPUs using advanced SVDQ or GGUF quantization.20 It excels in absolute photorealism, flawless typography, and multi-reference conditioning.17

FLUX.2 [klein]: Distilled 9B and 4B variants designed for sub-second inference on consumer hardware.22 The 4B model fits comfortably in 8-13GB VRAM, providing a vital bridge for local users.22

Regarding censorship, the official FLUX base models are "aligned" and feature minimal censorship. They rarely output direct text-based refusals, but their pre-training data was sanitized, meaning they lack the latent knowledge to generate accurate genitalia or extreme NSFW scenarios out of the box.1 Furthermore, the restrictive non-commercial licenses on the Dev variants prohibit the generation of legally sensitive content.5 To achieve true uncensored generation, the community relies heavily on models like FLUX-UNCENSORED-Merged or specific massive fine-tunes (such as Chroma1-HD or SNOFS) that forcibly overwrite the safety alignments via LoRAs or deep model surgery.24

Stable Diffusion 3.5 (SD3.5 Large / Medium) Released by Stability AI in late 2024 to rectify the widely criticized launch of SD3, SD3.5 Large (8.1B parameters) and Medium (2B parameters) feature permissive community licenses and excellent prompt adherence.27 However, as of May 2026, the SD3.5 architecture is largely viewed by the local generation community as deprecated.15 While technically easier to fully fine-tune than the distilled FLUX models, its base censorship profile is highly restrictive. Stability AI enforces this strict alignment via platform takedowns on CivitAI for any explicit content generated using SD3.5 derivatives.28 This aggressive moderation, coupled with a weaker aesthetic baseline compared to FLUX, prevented SD3.5 from capturing the uncensored market.28

SDXL 1.0 Base and Major Fine-Tunes Despite its age, the 2.6B parameter SDXL 1.0 architecture remains highly relevant in 2026 purely due to hardware economics. Users with 8GB VRAM GPUs who cannot run 32B FLUX models rely on SDXL fine-tunes.31 The base SDXL model contains partial censorship, heavily restricting explicit anatomy.1 However, years of community fine-tuning have completely shattered these guardrails. Major uncensored fine-tunes include Juggernaut XL (for cinematic realism), Epicrealism and Cyberrealistic (for photographic outputs), Lustify (for dedicated NSFW generation), and BigAsp-v2.5 (which integrates flow-matching techniques into the SDXL base).24

1.3 The Eastern Vanguard: Unrestricted Open Weights

The most significant geopolitical shift in the 2026 local AI landscape is the dominance of Chinese technology laboratories. These entities are releasing highly capable, completely uncensored foundation models under permissive Apache 2.0 licenses, directly challenging the restrictive practices of Western labs.

Z-Image Turbo & Zeta Chroma Released in late 2025 by Alibaba's Tongyi MAI lab, Z-Image is universally acclaimed as the premier local model for photorealism and general uncensored generation.1 Utilizing a novel Scalable Single-Stream Diffusion Transformer (S3-DiT), it processes text, visual semantic tokens, and VAE tokens in a single concatenated sequence, creating a highly efficient 6-billion parameter architecture.36 Z-Image Turbo distills this generation process to just 8 steps, achieving generation times of mere seconds on standard consumer GPUs (requiring 12–16GB VRAM).37 The model is completely uncensored out of the box, handling explicit concepts without any refusal mechanisms or latent anatomical gaps.1 A community derivative, Zeta Chroma (authored by Lodestone Rock), shifts the S3-DiT architecture into pixel space to further expand its artistic and stylistic versatility, though it remains in active development.39

Qwen-Image-2512 A massive 20B+ parameter model released by Alibaba-Qwen in late 2025, Qwen-Image integrates language and layout reasoning directly into the diffusion architecture.1 It is virtually unmatched in text rendering, typography, and complex prompt adherence.41 While its immense size natively requires over 40GB of VRAM in BF16 precision, the community has aggressively quantized it via GGUF formats, allowing it to run efficiently on 14GB–16GB consumer cards.42 Its censorship is categorized as minimal; while it does not actively refuse prompts, it generally requires light LoRA assistance to achieve explicit anatomical accuracy for extreme NSFW scenarios.1

ERNIE-Image Baidu's ERNIE-Image, released in 2026, competes directly at the frontier level of open-source image generation. It utilizes an 8B DiT architecture paired with a built-in Prompt Enhancer (the Ministral3ForCausalLM text encoder) that automatically expands short user inputs into complex, highly detailed spatial instructions.44 Released under an Apache 2.0 license with minimal censorship, it is highly regarded for structured layouts—such as comics, posters, and multi-panel compositions—and boasts extremely fast generation speeds in its Turbo variant.45

Playground v2.5 and v3 Playground v3 (PGv3) introduced significant architectural changes, opting for an "expand-PE" positional embedding rather than "interpolating-PE" to prevent the model from overfitting on specific training resolutions.47 It also upgraded the latent space by building an in-house 16-channel VAE (up from the traditional 4-channel VAEs), allowing for much finer detail synthesis in small faces and typography.47 However, Playground maintains a relatively strict censorship stance compared to its Eastern counterparts. The base models are heavily aligned, and generating uncensored content requires users to seek out specific community fine-tunes that have attempted to undo the safety training.48

Kwai Kolors and PixArt-Σ Kwai Kolors, developed by Kuaishou Technology, is a large-scale text-to-image model based on latent diffusion.49 While it produces exceptional aesthetics and highly stylized outputs, the community notes that it struggles with strict prompt adherence and extreme realism compared to DiT models.50 Furthermore, while open-source, its license requires commercial users to register formally, and it lacks natively uncensored variants.50 Conversely, PixArt-Σ (Sigma) is a highly efficient DiT model utilizing a T5 encoder capable of direct 4K resolution generation.16 With a much smaller footprint than Kolors, PixArt-Σ is favored by developers for its ease of training and fine-tuning, though the base model also requires community LoRAs to bypass baseline safety alignments for NSFW generation.52

HunyuanVideo 1.5 and HunyuanDiT Tencent's Hunyuan series dominates the open-source video generation sector. HunyuanVideo 1.5 (8.3B parameters) features a 3D causal VAE and a Selective and Sliding Tile Attention (SSTA) mechanism, which compresses spatial dimensions by 16x and temporal dimensions by 4x, drastically reducing computational overhead.53 While the base weights of the model are unrestricted, the official inference code provided by Tencent includes hardcoded safety filters and text-encoder blocklists. To achieve uncensored generation, the community relies heavily on "cosy" UI builds and patched workflows that strip out these safety classifiers, allowing for unrestricted NSFW video generation.55 Video generation remains extremely resource-intensive, requiring a minimum of 14GB VRAM with heavy offloading enabled, though 24GB is strongly recommended for high-fidelity outputs.53
