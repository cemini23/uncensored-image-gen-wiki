## Raw Concept

Question: What is the optimal corporate structure for a multi-persona AI/synthetic media company, and how does the Florida Series LLC enable horizontal liability isolation?

Synthesized from: @sources/synthetic-media-ip-financial-roadmap.md (entire Section 1), @sources/ai-creator-operations-blueprint.md (corporate structure sections).

## Narrative

### The Problem: Multi-Persona Liability Isolation

Operating an agency with multiple AI personas — each with distinct IP, audiences, brand deals, and operational liabilities — traditionally required forming **separate LLCs per persona**. This approach:

- Exponentially multiplies state filing fees and administrative overhead
- Creates complex inter-company accounting burdens
- Still leaves the **core AI model weights** (the most valuable asset) potentially exposed to litigation from any single persona's operations

### The Solution: Florida Protected Series LLC

The **Florida Uniform Protected Series Act** (CS/SB 316 and CS/HB 403), fully operable **July 1, 2026**, allows a single master LLC to create unlimited internal **"protected series"** — each functioning as a distinct legal person with its own assets, members, and managers.

#### Recommended Internal Taxonomy

| Entity | Role | What It Holds |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **Master LLC** | Central administration | Payroll, executive management; holds **no** direct IP or operational risk |
| **Protected Series A** — IP Holding Cell | Asset protection | Proprietary LoRA weights, training datasets, character bibles, trademark/copyright registrations |
| **Protected Series B** — Persona 1 Ops | Active operations | Social media accounts, brand deals, DTC revenue for Persona 1 |
| **Protected Series C** — Persona 2 Ops | Active operations | Same as Series B, fully segregated from Persona 1 |
| *(Additional series per persona as needed)* | | |

#### Horizontal Liability Shield

If Series B (Persona 1) faces litigation — e.g., copyright infringement on generated content or right of publicity dispute — the shield **legally prevents** plaintiffs from accessing:

- The core ML model weights in Series A
- Revenue generated by Series C or any other operational series

**Critical compliance requirement:** Florida law mandates completely separate financial records, dedicated bank accounts, and strict avoidance of fund commingling for each series. Violation can lead to **piercing of the corporate veil**.

### Tax Strategy

The IRS permits each protected series to elect its own tax treatment independently:

| Series | Recommended Election | Rationale |
|--------|---------------------|-----------|
| Series A (IP Holding) | Disregarded entity (default) or C-Corp | Simplicity; C-Corp if raising VC equity rounds |
| Series B, C (Ops) | **S-Corporation** | Minimizes self-employment tax on active income for founders |

**Florida state advantages:**
- **5.5% corporate income tax** on C-Corps only, with $50,000 baseline exemption
- **Pass-through entities (LLC/S-Corp) are entirely exempt** from state corporate income tax — revenue flows directly to personal federal returns
- **Florida R&D Tax Credit**: 10% credit on qualified research expenses exceeding a historical base amount; $9M annual cap; application window March 20–26; requires "qualified target industry business" certification from FL Dept of Commerce

### Regional Incentives (South Florida)

For agencies establishing physical presence in Broward County / Town of Davie:

- **Town of Davie CRA Commercial Property Improvement Program Grants**: $1 of municipal funding for every $3 spent on eligible construction, up to $100,000
- **Broward County Strategic Job Creation Incentive**: $750–$2,000 per new job, if wages exceed 115–200% of county average

**Stacking strategy:** Federal S-Corp election + State R&D credit + Municipal CRA grants can drastically reduce effective operational burn rate during incubation.
